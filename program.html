The Itinerary

A Parisian Bastille Day, day by day.

Your winning week follows the French Atelier signature program: “A Parisian Bastille Day Sojourn — Four Days as a Cultural Initiate.” This is the exact tour each winner experiences, July 12–15, 2026.

Sunday · July 12
01 Arrival · The Root of Paris

First Taste of the Marais

Settle into historic cobblestones & discover the revolutionary past

Check into a boutique hotel in the Marais, take your first café en terrasse, and walk the cobblestone streets. If you arrive before 3pm, visit the Musée Carnavalet to decipher the story of the French Revolution and why July 14 matters.

Hôtel du Petit Moulin Musée Carnavalet Les Philosophes Le Loir dans la Théière
Monday · July 13
02 Day 1 · The Golden Vigil

Classic Symmetry & Symphonic Fireworks

Place des Vosges, a golden Seine sunset, and the national fireworks

Begin at the elegant Place des Vosges and Île de la Cité, then board a classic boat for a golden-hour Seine cruise past the Louvre and Grand Palais. In the evening: the Concert de Paris at the Champ-de-Mars and the national fireworks at 23:00, with the Eiffel Tower ablaze.

Place des Vosges Seine Sunset Cruise Concert de Paris Eiffel Fireworks · 23:00

⚠ Note: in 2026 the national fireworks fall on Monday evening, July 13. Dine early — the sun sets late in July.

Tuesday · July 14 · Bastille Day
03 ★ The Main Event Day 2 · Parisian Elegance

Air Shows, Belle Époque & Grand Opera

The insider’s approach to July 14

Arrive early near Concorde and the Jardin des Tuileries for the military air-show flyover — skipping the Champs-Élysées crowds. Continue to the Palais-Royal and the gilded Opéra Garnier, then take in the rooftop terraces of Boulevard Haussmann.

Tuileries Air Show Palais-Royal Opéra Garnier Café de la Paix
Wednesday · July 15
04 Day 3 · Art, Light & Water

Slow Montmartre & Impressionist Masters

Sacré-Cœur, the Musée d’Orsay, and Seine guinguettes

A slow morning in Montmartre — the Moulin de la Galette and Sacré-Cœur — then the Impressionist masters at the Musée d’Orsay (Monet, Renoir, Van Gogh). End on the water at a riverside guinguette, with a glass of cold French wine as the city slows.

Sacré-Cœur Musée d'Orsay Seine Guinguette Riverside Flânerie
