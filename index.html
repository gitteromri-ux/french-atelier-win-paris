Your Itinerary

Four days, planned for you.

The full program winners will live, July 12–15, 2026. Tap any day for the complete plan — neighborhoods, museums, cruises and where to dine.

Sunday, July 12

Day 1 · The Marais

See the full July 12 plan+
  • Boutique check-in — a Marais boutique hotel: Hôtel du Petit Moulin, Maison Proust or Caron de Beaumarchais.
  • First café en terrasse — linger and watch neighborhood life unfold.
  • Musée Carnavalet — the city's history and the deep story of Bastille Day.
Recommended gems
Les Philosophes · Le Bon Georges · Au Bourguignon du Marais · Le Loir dans la Théière
Monday, July 13

Day 2 · The Golden Vigil

See the full July 13 plan+
  • Place des Vosges & Île de la Cité — Paris's oldest square, the Renaissance gardens, the river banks.
  • Golden sunset cruise — board at 17:30 as the July light softens over the Louvre and Orsay.
  • Concert de Paris & national fireworks — at 23:00 the fireworks set the Eiffel Tower ablaze.
Note
In 2026 the national fireworks fall on Monday evening, July 13. We dine early as the sun sets late.
Tuesday, July 14

Day 3 · Parisian Elegance

See the full July 14 plan+
  • The insider's parade approach — skip the crowds; watch the air-show flyover from the Tuileries at 8:30.
  • Opéra Garnier & rooftops — the marble staircases, then panoramic views from Galeries Lafayette.
  • High art & opera dining — an evening of spectacle and legendary brasseries.
Recommended gems
Palais Garnier Ballet · Comédie-Française · Café de la Paix · Bouillon Chartier
Wednesday, July 15

Day 4 · Art, Light & Water

See the full July 15 plan+
  • Slow Montmartre — cobblestone streets from Abbesses, the Moulin de la Galette and Sacré-Cœur.
  • Light at Orsay — Monet, Renoir, Manet, Degas and Van Gogh in the great Impressionist hall.
  • Seine-side guinguette — a cold glass of French wine as the city slows on the water's edge.
Recommended gems
Le Moulin de la Galette · Marcel · Hardware Société · a riverside guinguette
