Day 1 · First Taste of the Marais

Settle into historical cobblestones and discover the city's revolutionary past.

  • Boutique Check-In — a Marais boutique hotel: Hôtel du Petit Moulin, Maison Proust, or Hôtel Caron de Beaumarchais.

Monday, July 13 — Classic Symmetry, Seine Sunset & Symphonic Fireworks

Day 2 · The Golden Vigil

An elegant transition from Renaissance cobblestones to water-side music and lights.
See the full July 13 itineraryExpandCollapse
1 · Morning Splendours
  • Place des Vosges — the perfect symmetry of Paris's oldest square.
  • Hôtel de Sully — through the secret Renaissance gardens.
  • Île de la Cité — the banks to Notre-Dame & Square Jean XXIII.
  • Terrasse Lunch — Robert et Louise, GrandCœur, Café Charlot, or Au Bourguignon.
2 · Golden Sunset Cruise
  • Late-Afternoon Cruise — board a classic boat at 17:30–18:00 as the July light softens.
  • River Architecture — the Louvre, Orsay & Grand Palais in gold rays.
  • Early Alma Dining — Monsieur Bleu, Francette, or Café de l'Alma.
3 · Fireworks & Music
  • Concert de Paris — the magnificent outdoor orchestra at Champ-de-Mars.
  • National Fireworks — at 23:00, the fireworks set the Eiffel Tower ablaze.
  • Festive Picnics — classical notes, champagne & local families under the stars.

Note: the National Fireworks are exceptionally scheduled for Monday evening, July 13 in 2026. We dine early as the sun sets late in July.

Tuesday, July 14 — Air Shows, Belle Époque Spire & the Grand Opera

Day 3 · Parisian Elegance

A masterclass in navigating National Day celebrations like a true Parisian insider.
See the full July 14 itineraryExpandCollapse
The Insider's Parade Approach
  • Sentinels of the Sky — skip the dense Champs-Élysées crowds; arrive early (8:30) near Concorde or the Tuileries.
  • Jardin des Tuileries — peaceful green alleys to watch the military air-show flyover.
  • Palais-Royal — the Buren columns & Comédie-Française; lunch at Le Grand Colbert or Bistrot Vivienne.
Belle Époque & Rooftops
  • Opéra Garnier — the sweeping marble staircases & golden details of Palais Garnier.
  • Boulevard Haussmann — the rooftop terraces of Galeries Lafayette / Printemps for panoramic views.
  • Opening Caution — grands-boulevards shops are often open even on National Day; check times close to the date.
High Art & Opera Dining
  • Savor an evening of high culture — spectacle choices & legendary brasseries for Tuesday evening.
Recommended Gems
Palais Garnier Ballet · Comédie-Française · Coco Palais Garnier · Café de la Paix · Bouillon Chartier
Wednesday, July 15 — Slow Montmartre, Impressionist Masterpieces & Seine Guinguettes

Day 4 · Art, Light & Water

Entering the ultimate 'French Atelier' moments of emotional light and lingering conversations.
See the full July 15 itineraryExpandCollapse
1 · Slow Montmartre
  • Poetic Wandering — the quiet charm of cobblestone streets, starting at Métro Abbesses.
  • Moulin & Sacré-Cœur — the iconic Moulin de la Galette and views from Sacré-Cœur.
  • Lamarck-Caulaincourt — the tranquil northern stairways, far from tourist buses.
  • Artistic Lunch — Le Moulin de la Galette, Marcel, or Hardware Société.
2 · Light at Orsay
  • Impressionist Masters — Monet, Renoir, Manet, Degas & Van Gogh at Musée d'Orsay.
  • A Cultural Revolution — Impressionism as an emotional revolution of light & everyday modern life.
  • Artistic Conversation — the bold paintstrokes & soft sunset tones of the 19th-century avant-garde.
3 · Seine-Side Linger
  • Riverside Flânerie — the historic stone banks of the Seine, ripples catching gold light.
  • Guinguette Evenings — conclude at an authentic riverside guinguette / terrace.
  • Lingering Light — a cold glass of French wine as the city slows. Linger, converse, breathe the warm air.

Summer days linger in Paris. We end our day slow, on the edge of the water, where the sunset catches the ripples of history.

Your Questions, Answered

Contest FAQ.

How do I qualify to win?

Follow French Atelier on Instagram/Meta, share our channel link with your network (a minimum of 10 shares — channel-link messages sent), capture a screenshot showing those 10+ shares, and submit the short entry form. The three participants who share the most — and bring in the most engaged new community — win.

What exactly do I need to screenshot?

A print-screen showing at least 10 shares — the channel-link messages you actually sent to friends, family and your network. This is your proof of entry, and a minimum of 10 is required to qualify for the win.

How are the three winners selected?

A committee reviews shared activity on social, new channel followers linked to your shares, and overall engagement level. The top three sharers, meeting the 10-share minimum, are selected.

What does the prize include?

A round-trip flight from a major US airport to Paris, timed for Bastille Day, plus access to our complete three-day French Atelier insider program of terrasses, hidden gems, museums, cruises and curated dining.

When can I fly?

Outbound flights from the USA are valid for July 10, 11 or 12; return flights through July 13, 14 or 15. Any booking changes are valid for the month of July only, at an additional $80 per ticket.

How and when will I be notified if I win?

Winners are contacted by phone call, text message and email. If a contacted winner does not respond within 7 days, the prize is forfeited and transferred to an alternative winner.
