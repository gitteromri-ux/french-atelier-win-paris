The Itinerary

Three days, lived like a Parisian.

Each winner experiences the French Atelier signature program — “A Parisian Bastille Day Sojourn: 3 Days as a Cultural Initiate.” This is the exact tour, July 13–15, 2026.

01
Day 1 · Monday, July 13 · The Golden Vigil

Classic Symmetry & Symphonic Fireworks

Place des Vosges, a golden Seine sunset, and the national fireworks

Begin at the perfect symmetry of Place des Vosges and the Île de la Cité. In the late afternoon, board a classic boat for a golden-hour Seine cruise past the Louvre, Orsay, and Grand Palais. In the evening: the Concert de Paris at the Champ-de-Mars, then the national fireworks at 23:00 with the Eiffel Tower ablaze.

Place des Vosges Seine Sunset Cruise Concert de Paris National Fireworks · 23:00

⚠ In 2026 the national fireworks fall on Monday evening, July 13. We dine early — the sun sets late in July.

02
★ The Main Event · Bastille Day Day 2 · Tuesday, July 14 · Official Grandeur

Air Shows, Belle Époque & Grand Opera

The insider’s approach to France’s National Day

Arrive early (8:30) near Concorde and the Jardin des Tuileries for the military air-show flyover — skipping the dense Champs-Élysées crowds. Continue to the Palais-Royal and the gilded Opéra Garnier, then take in the rooftop terraces of Boulevard Haussmann.

Tuileries Air Show Palais-Royal Opéra Garnier Café de la Paix
03
Day 3 · Wednesday, July 15 · Art, Light & Water

Slow Montmartre & Impressionist Masters

Sacré-Cœur, the Musée d’Orsay, and Seine guinguettes

A slow morning in Montmartre — the Moulin de la Galette and Sacré-Cœur — then the Impressionist masters at the Musée d’Orsay (Monet, Renoir, Manet, Van Gogh). End on the water at a riverside guinguette, with a cold glass of French wine as the city slows and the light lingers.

Sacré-Cœur Musée d’Orsay Seine Guinguette Riverside Flânerie
